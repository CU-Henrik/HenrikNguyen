Bio

Hello! My name is Henrik Nguyen, and I am a junior studying Mechanical Engineering at the University of Colorado Boulder. I enjoy hands-on work, especially turning CAD designs into real, physical builds. I am interested in applying engineering to real-world systems, and after graduation I hope to work in aerospace, defense, or robotics where I can design and improve complex systems.

I have always liked figuring out how things work. Whether it was taking apart objects, understanding systems in games, or solving everyday problems, that curiosity naturally led me to engineering. A defining moment for me was taking a CAD class in high school, where I was able to design something digitally and then physically create it. Seeing that process from concept to reality made me realize this was the field I wanted to pursue.

Since then, I have continued building on that interest through hands-on projects. I started with things like trebuchets and gear-driven cars, and now work on larger projects like drill-powered bikes, windmills, and fabrication-based builds in college. I also have an upcoming internship this summer at Mead & Hunt, where I will work as a Mechanical Engineering Intern. In this role, I will contribute to building systems such as HVAC while collaborating with engineers from different disciplines.

Looking ahead, my goal is to graduate, complete my master’s through the BAM program, and become a professional engineer. I want to work in an industry I am passionate about and continue gaining experience through projects, internships, and new opportunities. What excites me most about engineering is the creativity involved. Even when problems have a single correct answer, there are many ways to approach them. Being able to design, iterate, and improve solutions is what makes the work interesting to me.
